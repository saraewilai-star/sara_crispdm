CRISP-DM Data Science Methodology

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) is a widely used data science methodology that provides a structured and systematic approach for planning, developing, and deploying data-driven solutions.

Chosen Methodology: CRISP-DM

CRISP-DM is a standard methodology for developing data science projects in a step-by-step and well-organized manner.

Research Methodology

Definition & Origin

CRISP-DM was introduced in 1996 by IBM, SPSS, and Daimler-Benz to provide a standard framework for data mining projects.

Key Phases

  1. Business Understanding – Define business objectives.
  2. Data Understanding – Collect and explore data.
  3. Data Preparation – Clean and prepare data.
  4. Modeling – Build predictive models.
  5. Evaluation – Evaluate model performance.
  6. Deployment – Apply results in real-world use.

Purpose & Applications

  • Used when projects involve large datasets and data-driven decision making
  • Common industries: retail, healthcare, banking, marketing

Advantages

  • Clear and structured process
  • Easy to understand
  • Widely used in industry

Limitations

  • Less flexible for real-time / streaming data
  • Limited focus on engineering deployment details

Real-World Example: Customer Churn Prediction (Telecommunications)

Problem: Predict customers likely to cancel subscriptions (churn).

Phase Explanation
Business Understanding Reduce customer churn rate
Data Understanding Collect customer data: age, usage, monthly bill, contract type
Data Preparation Remove missing values, normalize/scale values
Modeling Build Logistic Regression model
Evaluation Measure accuracy/performance (e.g., confusion matrix)
Deployment Marketing team targets high-risk customers for retention

Final Output: A list of customers with high churn risk used for retention strategies.
